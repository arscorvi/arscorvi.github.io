#### OVERVIEW
> **AGE**: 27-years-old\
> **RACE**: Human\
> **GENDER**: Male\
> **PROFESSION**: Medical Doctor\
> **BACKGROUND**: Hot-headed Lapdog

A tempermental man most often seen working within the station's medical department. Fiercely loyal to Nanotrasen and its interests, he can be a little unforgiving when it comes to matters of protocol and workplace security and is often angered by his colleague's reckless behaviour. This underlying temper, however, is little more than a reluctant mask; in truth, he's soft, caring, and compassionate, and will freely throw himself in harm's way for the sake of others.

#### APPEARANCE
> **HEIGHT**: 5'6"\
> **WEIGHT**: ~130lbs\
> **SKIN COLOUR**: Tanned\
> **HAIR COLOUR**: Dark Garnet\
> **EYE COLOUR**: Olive Green

True to his namesake, Levi bears many features that evoke heat, warmth, and fire; his skin is well-tanned even with limited exposure to sunlight, and his hair is fashioned into a rough ponytail, rising in places with unruly wisps and strands — like little flames — that are a shade or two lighter than the rest.

He prefers practical, stylish attire (though his sense of what is and is not practical is skewed heavily towards formal wear) and, in spite of his at times literal hot-headedness, he frequently complains of being too cold, layering up with whatever jackets and winter coats he can get his hands on.

#### QUIRKS

Keeps a first-edition copy of *Surgical Ethics: Principles and Practice* on his nightstand; can smoke a cigarette in forty seconds flat; sometimes bursts into flames yet remains wholly unsurprised by this fact; is lactose intolerant but refuses to give up drinking milk, even if it's 'spicy'; responsible for many of the dents in the walls of the medical department from headbutting them in moments of frustration; growls and barks, sometimes.

#### BIOGRAPHY
The circumstances surrounding Levi's origins are shrouded in a wealth of red tape, corporate jargon, and heavily redacted papers and documentation; there's little that remains of a paper trail prior to his employment with Nanotrasen, and even he, himself, remains largely unaware of the exact timeline of events, barring a few sparse, fleeting details.

When asked, he will gladly offer up a story of an 'ordinary' upbringing, but the more he explains, the less ordinary it begins to seem; a lot of his time was spent in and out of various medical departments, universities, and research outposts. Memories of glass-walled rooms, people in labcoats and uniforms, and a routine that was structured around various tests and seemingly random, unimportant activities.

As he grew older, however, these tests became increasingly less frequent, and he was left with an abundance of free time and a distinct, overwhelming lack of purpose. For a time, he considered himself forgotten; the 'suits' came to see him much more rarely during these years, leaving him in the care of the select few he had grown up with: researchers, mostly, judging by the fact that their noses were constantly buried in paperwork.

Eventually, the tests and activities stopped altogether, and Levi was to do what everyone else was expected to do — work. With little in the way of guidance, and with the doors to Nanotrasen already being open to him through what was undoubtedly a subsidiary, he chose a career — Medicine — at random and signed his contract without much thought.

Already being innately familiar with the hustle and bustle of medical environments and the relentless workflow of study and research, he adapted well enough, keeping his head down — as he had already done for so many years — and eventually graduated as a Medical Doctor. From there, it was only a matter of a few more signatures, a few more dotted lines, and he was shipped out into the Frontier with an identification card and a small handful of credits.

Since then, he's done little but work tirelessly without rest. There's scant few memories to act as a contrast to his life as a corporate employee and, as a result, he has dedicated himself wholly to his employment and remains unquestioningly loyal to his position within Nanotrasen.

#### CROW'S NOTES
<div class="box">
Levi Ignis is a character that I shamelessly adapted from the quartet of elemental gods/deities that make up the pantheon of one of my older worldbuilding exercises; with a name like <i>Ignis</i>, it should come as no surprise that he's styled after the god of fire, and this influenced a great deal of his character. His temperament, his palette, and his overall themes are a direct result of this, but it was something of a challenge to adapt the idea from a fantasy setting to science fiction.

At first, he went through a few iterations — chiefly about his origins, his age, and other foundational details — before I settled on his current design. He soon ended up becoming a fully-fledged character in his own right, and I quickly abandoned any references to his other elemental siblings; <i>Aquae</i>, <i>Terra</i>, and <i>Venti</i>, though they may one day get their own profiles.

Unlike my other Space Station 13 characters, he's much more aligned with how I typically write characters and role-play them. His personality is designed in such a way as to be largely immutable, influencing how I play the game and how he interacts with others, with little of the flexibility that comes with more non-specific characters. Having a strong personality to refer to certainly helps.

As with all of my characters, he isn't perfect, but he's reached the stage that I always aim for and so very often fall short of; any and all of his continued development and design can happen in-game, through interactions, role-play, and storytelling. I'm confident with what I've written here as a framework. The rest will come naturally.
