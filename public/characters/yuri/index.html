SS13 — Yuri Stratos

OVERVIEW

AGE: 27-years-old
RACE: Human
GENDER: Male
PROFESSION: Medical Doctor/Director
BACKGROUND: Space-born Academic

A soft-spoken man with a passion for medical sciences and research; largely reserved and well-mannered, he’s quietly content with both his life and his working environment and demonstrates a strict adherence to protocol and ethics. Though situations outside of his control often push him outside of his comfort zone, he remains resolute in his desire to keep his colleagues and the people around him safe and sound.

APPEARANCE

HEIGHT: 5'8"
WEIGHT: ~140lbs
SKIN COLOUR: Albino
HAIR COLOUR: Platinum
EYE COLOUR: Light Pink/Red

Owing to his albinism, Yuri’s appearance is notably pale and lacking in pigmentation; his skin is almost alabaster in colour, and he bears the condition’s telltale red eyes. His hair — a warm shade of platinum — is rarely ever cut, leaving him with a well-groomed mane that frames his face in such a way as to obscure most of his features.

He typically dresses in more formal attire than is necessary, replacing his standard-issue scrubs with buttoned shirts, neutral slacks, and waistcoats, though he’s still partial to the signature white labcoats of the medical department.

QUIRKS

Has never set foot on solid ground and doesn’t intend to change that fact any time soon; keeps an extensive and well-stocked liquor cabinet in his apartment and often moonlights as a bartender; will make light of dangerous situations in a poor attempt at coping with the stress; will never divulge the secret of which shampoo brand he uses, even under duress; learned how to play the piano whilst studying at university, though sometimes he forgets which pedal is which.

BIOGRAPHY

Hailing from a family of scientists and academics, Yuri Stratos is a young man born during the peak of space exploration; his parents, both from the United Kingdom, were happy to leave the Earth behind for the alluring embrace of the stars, even if it meant raising their only son amongst cold steel, endlessly whirring machinery, and simulated gravity.

In spite of these circumstances, much of Yuri’s early life was unremarkable, and he never felt a longing for, nor attachment to, the pale blue dot that his family came from. As he grew older, he steadily became more and more interested in the work that his parents were performing in the pursuit of further exploration and colonization, and found himself rapidly abandoning many of his childhood dreams and ideals for more grounded pursuits; of all of the options available to him, he eventually settled on the field of medicine, and enrolled in university shortly thereafter.

What followed were years of intensive study and a total surrender of himself to the demands of academia; an oddly feverish desire to prove his worth (undoubtedly from having such high-achieving parents) had him giving every ounce of energy possible to his chosen career, leaving him with little in the way of a social life. For the duration of his studies, and for many years afterwards, he could count the entirety of his close friendships on the fingers of a single hand, and his relationship with his parents similarly suffered, especially with the discovery of the Channel; having already abandoned Earth, they were equally swift to abandon the solar system as a whole for the Frontier and disappeared, with little in the way of communication, as far into space as possible.

With increasingly fewer ties to any singular place, it was unsurprising that, as soon as he had graduated from university, Yuri began looking towards corporate sponsorships and residencies. Feeling little to no attachment to the various ships and stations that he had grown accustomed to — along with impressive test scores, practical knowledge, and a familiar comfort with being a functioning cog — made him an ideal candidate for Nanotrasen’s already solid grasp on space travel and research within the Frontier.

It’s been a few years since then, and he’s adapted well enough to corporate life. The long shifts and frequent crises take their toll, as they would with anyone, but the sense of purpose his position affords him, and the growing bonds with his fellow crew — unfamiliar, but not unwelcome — both have him comfortably calling the Frontier his home.

CROW’S NOTES

I made Yuri Stratos after returning to Space Station 13's Goonstation server after a brief hiatus, and he was never intended to be anything more than an easily identifiable avatar. I picked the name from a mixture of sources: his given name was taken from Yuri Gagarin, the Soviet cosmonaut (in spite of the character not being Russian in the slightest), whilst his surname was shamelessly lifted from Mobile Suit Gundam 00. I couldn't come up with anything remarkably unique when designing him, owing to the fact that there was very little intent behind the design to begin with, so I opted for the oft overused cliché of making him albino.

What ended up happening, however, is that Yuri quickly became the default face of the personae I play in Goonstation, and remains to-date the one character that I feel most connected to and am most consistently associated with. A lot of this is due to the fact that it was only after that aforementioned hiatus that I began to earnestly engage with the community on a more personal level — participating more openly, making connections, and the like — and that, by not giving my character a particularly restrictive playstyle or background, it was possible for me to engage with more of the game’s systems and mechanics than I had done previously.

Chief amongst these mechanics was the game’s medical department. Beforehand, I had almost exclusively played as either a Security Assistant or a Security Officer, and Yuri’s original design (including some of his artwork) reflects this. In allowing myself to be fluid with him, my own preferences and enjoyment have come to shape the character more and more, leading me to rework him in his entirety towards his current and likely final iteration; a Medical Doctor.

There are still some things that I’m unhappy with; he’s supposed to be soft-spoken, to be well-mannered, and he leans heavily towards being more serious in nature, but there are often times where I get caught up in the moment, forget these things, and give the impression of a character that is wholly unserious and comedic. It’s easy enough to dismiss these moments as non-canon, but it’s a peculiar problem that, so far, has been exclusively to my time spent on Goonstation.

On the whole, though, I’m happy with how he turned out and how I play him. It’s been a refreshing experience to allow myself to be more synchronous with one of my characters instead of distancing myself wholly from them.
